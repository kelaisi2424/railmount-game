A Quick Overview of Railmount

Railmount gives you a grid, at least one train, a starting station, and an end station. Your job is to click and drag rails between those points so the locomotive can chug from A to B without derailing. It sounds simple, and the first five or six puzzles genuinely are. What makes this Railmount walkthrough necessary is how quickly Hempuli layers new rules on top of that foundation — suddenly you have two trains crossing paths, one-way arrow tiles, or stations that must be visited in a specific order.

Rather than spoil the exact rail layout of every puzzle, this Railmount guide breaks the game into three difficulty tiers and explains the concepts you need to solve each tier. If you are hunting for Railmount solutions to a specific level, scroll to the section that matches the mechanic introduced there. Early Levels — Learning the Rails

The opening stages of Railmount teach the click-and-drag rail mechanic. You will see a single train, a clear start tile, and a single destination. The fastest way through the early game is to trace the most direct path first and then add curves only where walls or obstacles force you to bend around them. If you accidentally draw a rail into a dead end, click the rail tile again to erase it — no need to restart the whole level.

Pay attention to the level intro screens. Hempuli is famous for teaching mechanics without words, and almost every new tile type gets one "tutorial" puzzle before the game expects you to combine it with older ideas. If a level introduces a glowing crossroad tile, for example, the level right after it will usually require two rails to cross through that same tile. Treat early levels as pattern study, not just warm-ups.

Mid-Game Levels — Multiple Trains & Timing

Once you reach the mid-game, most puzzles feature two or three trains that have to reach separate destinations on the same grid. The golden rule here is plan the hardest route first. Usually one train has a long, twisting path, while the others have short straight runs. If you design the simple trains first, you will almost always block the path of the complex one. Solve backward: sketch the longest path, then weave the shorter ones around it.

Timing also starts to matter. Railmount trains move simultaneously when you press play, so two trains crossing the same junction tile will collide unless they arrive at different moments. Add small detour loops to the faster train so it arrives one or two ticks later. These delay loops are the main Railmount solutions trick for puzzles in tier two, and recognizing when a level needs a loop saves hours of trial and error.

Late Game — Directional Tiles & Advanced Layouts

The final Railmount levels introduce directional arrow tiles, locked rail segments you cannot remove, and order-sensitive stations. This is where the Railmount walkthrough becomes less about exact rail drawings and more about reading the board. Start by listing every fixed element you cannot change. Those are the anchors that dictate the shape of your solution. Next, trace each train's required stops in order and mark the tiles it must pass through.

For puzzles with one-way arrows, remember that arrows also block trains travelling against them. If a train must return to its start station after a delivery, you will need a loop that respects every arrow on the route. Late-game Railmount solutions almost always involve at least one figure-eight or lollipop-shaped loop, so if your brain is hurting trying to route trains straight, try a curve that doubles back on itself.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ×Drawing rails before reading the board. Always count trains, stations, and arrow tiles first. Most failed Railmount runs come from missing a hidden second train.
  • ×Ignoring station order. Some levels require a train to visit stations in a strict sequence. A train that reaches the correct tiles in the wrong order still fails the level.
  • ×Using the shortest path for every train. In multi-train puzzles, the shortest path is often the blocker. Give one train the scenic route and save the direct route for a train that cannot detour.
  • ×Forgetting the play button. You can edit your rails freely, but once you press play, watch carefully for where trains stall or collide. Those tiles are the clue for your next attempt.

Advanced Railmount Techniques

Once you are comfortable, try these power-user tactics. First, use the grid corners. Corner tiles can often be reached by only one train, making them safe parking spots for delay loops. Second, solve each puzzle twice — once for correctness, once for elegance. Railmount rewards clever, compact solutions with a satisfying animation at the end, and retracing your steps deepens your intuition for later levels.

Third, take notes on any tile type that stumps you and revisit it after a break. Hempuli's puzzle design is notoriously pattern-based, and seeing the same tile two or three puzzles apart usually makes the underlying rule click. Walkthrough FAQ

How many levels are in Railmount?

Railmount contains dozens of hand-designed puzzles that get progressively harder. The game is divided into an early section that teaches core rail-drawing mechanics, a middle section that adds multiple trains and obstacles, and a late-game section with advanced branching logic.

What should I do if I get stuck on a Railmount level?

When stuck, clear the board and start over by mapping endpoints first, then the shortest rail between each pair. Look for choke points where only one train can pass at a time and plan the other routes around them. Most Railmount puzzles have more than one valid solution, so experiment freely.

Does this Railmount walkthrough contain spoilers?

This guide focuses on strategy and concepts rather than spelling out the exact rail layout for each puzzle. We group levels by difficulty tier so you can find help for the section you are on without spoiling later puzzles.
